How To Maintain And Care For Paint Protection Film

One of the best decisions you can make to protect your car paint from scratches, rock chips, and general environmental damage is to use Paint Protection Film (PPF). PPF can last a long time, but some of its properties, such as a glossy finish, are bound to fade over time, so some maintenance is required for it to perform its best. 

In this guide, Pure Finish Auto Detailing will show expert tips and techniques to help you extend your PPF’s life and ensure that your car is well-protected for many years. Whether you are completely unfamiliar with PPF or want to enhance your current routine, this article will be useful to you.

What is Paint Protection Film?

Paint Protection Film (PPF) is a transparent urethane film applied to a vehicle’s painted surfaces to provide an additional layer of protection. It acts as a barrier against various damage, such as scratches, stone chips, bug splatter, and environmental elements like UV rays and harsh weather conditions. 

PPF is typically optically clear, allowing the paint color and finish to shine without distortion. It is commonly used on areas of the vehicle that are prone to damage, such as the front bumper, hood, side mirrors, and door edges. Overall, PPF helps to preserve a vehicle’s appearance and value by safeguarding its paintwork from wear and tear.

What are the benefits of Paint Protection Film?

The major benefit of paint protection film is protecting automotive paint from the elements. Aside from this essential benefit, PPF offers additional benefits, which we will explore.

Enhancing resale value

Automotive paint is one of the most important factors affecting a vehicle’s appearance and resale value. Keeping this paint in good condition helps the car look well-maintained and cared for, which many used car buyers appreciate without worrying about repainting.

Maintaining aesthetic appeal

Unlike other protective measures that may alter a vehicle’s appearance, PPF is virtually invisible when properly installed. It allows the paint color and finish to shine without distortion, ensuring a seamless look.

Shielding against damage

PPF acts like a protective layer, adding an extra layer against various things that can harm your car. This protection is particularly crucial for vulnerable areas of the vehicle, such as the front bumper, hood, and side mirrors, which are more prone to damage from debris and environmental factors. Therefore, PPF ensures that your vehicle’s exterior remains flawless and well-maintained for years.

How do you take care of Paint Protection Film?

After applying PPF to your car, you would believe it requires no maintenance or care because it is so long-lasting. However, PPF, like everything else, is durable and not invincible, so it needs to be cared for. The methods outlined here will help you maintain your PPF.

Regular washing

Here are some steps to help you wash your car more efficiently.

  • Frequency: Wash your car at least every two weeks, or more often.
  • Technique: Use a pH-neutral car wash soap (PPF wash) and a microfiber wash mitt. 
  • Avoid using rough materials: Do not use abrasive sponges, terry cloth, or brushes that can scratch the film while washing.
  • Rinse thoroughly: Ensure all soap is rinsed away to prevent water spots.

Drying

Use a clean, soft microfiber towel to thoroughly dry your car. Avoid using terry cloth towels, as they can leave lint or scratches.

Avoid harsh chemicals

Avoid using bug and tar removers. These treatments may sound appealing, but they might contain harsh chemicals that may harm PPF. If you use it. Here is how it’s done: Take the microfiber and spray a bit of tar remover on your microfiber, rub it in, and let it sit for a couple of seconds; rub it off and rinse immediately. Don’t let tar remover sit on PPF.

Spot cleaning

When you have contamination like tree sap or bird droppings on your PPF that you don’t get off with the PPF wash, use PPF renew. PPF renew contains silica, not only does it restore gloss and slickness, but it also provides great UV protection and chemical resistance.

Periodic inspection

Inspect your PPF regularly for signs of deterioration, such as cuts, tears, or lifted edges. If you are unsure how to inspect visually, call your local detailer specializing in PPF to schedule an annual inspection. They can identify possible problems and recommend maintenance or fixes.

Professional cleaning and detailing

Professional PPF decon and ceramic coatings are best suited once every six months. The service cleans out any embedded contaminants, leaving the film sparkling, and also adds another layer of protection with ceramic coatings. The PPF is treated with ceramic coatings that become hydrophobic, making it easier to clean your vehicle while adding to the gloss and longevity of the finish.
